**Story bible review & revision (user feedback)**: Incorporated specific changes requested:

- Selection mechanism: Changed from "marked families / old blood" to a random lottery or "Community Harmony Score" based on parental contributions (work, loyalty, volunteering). This makes the horror feel more arbitrary and systemic.
- Ritual location: Moved from stone circle in the woods to the village playground (a place children are *supposed* to feel safe and happy).
- Visual/atmosphere elements: Removed white robes. Now "Playtime Guardians" — creepy clowns in bright but off costumes, oversized stitched stuffed animals, and living toys that move unnaturally during the rite.
- Villager perception: They do not notice the escape. They believe the ritual succeeded perfectly and "the Hunger is fed, all is well." The village proceeds normally at first.
- Escalation: Because the Hunger was not satisfied, strange things start happening (withering patches, restless dead, increased monster activity). Villagers panic and start running the lottery more often + making extra sacrifices, creating more monsters and accelerating the cycle's collapse.

Updated sections in docs/story/bible.md (Background, Cycle & The Hunger, Act 0, relevant twists). Lightly adjusted docs/characters/companions.md (Briar rescue). These changes make the inciting incident and early game more insidious and psychologically isolating for Rowan.
